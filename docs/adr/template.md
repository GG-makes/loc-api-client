# ADR NNNN: <short title stating the decision, not the problem>

Status: Proposed
Date: YYYY-MM-DD

## Context
The forces at play — the problem, the constraints, what made a decision
necessary. Write it so a reader with no memory of the situation understands the
pressure. State facts, not the choice yet.

## Decision
What we chose, stated plainly and in the active voice: "We do X." One decision
per ADR.

## Consequences
What follows — the benefits, the costs, and what is now constrained. Include the
uncomfortable parts: what becomes harder, what known limitation we accept, what
future decision this defers. Cross-reference related ADRs (e.g. "see ADR 0002").

## Alternatives considered
Each option we rejected and the reason. Often the most valuable section — it
stops people re-proposing dead ends.
